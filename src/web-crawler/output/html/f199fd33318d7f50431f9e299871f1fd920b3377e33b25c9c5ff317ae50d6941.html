Gamery is the all-in-one game tracker, collection manager, and gaming companion that helps you organize, explore, and stay on top of your video game universe. Whether you’re a casual gamer, a hardcore collector, or just tired of forgetting what’s in your backlog, Gamery gives you the tools to bring order and joy to your gaming life. Track your entire game collection Add digital and physical games to your library. Create custom collections, sort and filter by platform, genre, or play status, and always know what’s in your library. Import your games automatically Connect with Steam, PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo, GOG, Epic Games Store, and RetroAchievements. Gamery pulls your games, artwork, metadata, achievements, and progress — all in one place. Barcode scanning with ScanDex Have physical games? Use ScanDex, the most advanced video game barcode database, to scan and add them in seconds. Stay on top of your gaming goals Track your progress with statuses like “Playing,” “Completed,” or “Ongoing.” Add your own ratings, reviews, and notes. Know what you’ve played — and what’s next. Manage your backlog like a pro Gamery helps you see how many games you haven’t finished, which genres dominate your collection, and how long your “pile of shame” really is. Discover deep stats about your habits See which platforms you use most, which genres you prefer, how many games you finished this year, and more with beautiful visual stats. View personalized Yearly Replays Get a summary of your gaming activity year by year. See what you’ve played, how often, and how your collection has evolved. Get smart suggestions & reminders Not sure what to play? Let Gamery pick a random game from your backlog. Get notified about new releases, livestreams, and gaming events so you never miss out. Wishlist & event calendar Add upcoming titles to your wishlist and browse all upcoming releases. Gamery keeps track of announcements, release dates, and post-event summaries. Customize everything Choose from multiple home screen icons, app themes, and custom game page designs. Make Gamery feel like your own. Widgets for your Home Screen Display your current games, your wishlist, or collections, right on your Home Screen. Clean, beautiful design Gamery puts presentation first. Each game page features animated 3D artwork, layered headers, and a screenshot stack that lets you explore your games in style. Fully synced with iCloud Your entire library, settings, and data stay updated across all your Apple devices. No ads. Ever. Gamery is built for gamers, not advertisers. You won’t see pop-ups, banners, or tracking scripts. Gamery+ Features Gamery is free to download and use, but you can unlock the full experience with Gamery+. Premier gives you access to every feature, customization option, and premium update — with no limitations. Choose what works for you: • One-time purchase • Monthly subscription • Yearly subscription All purchases help support indie development and continued updates. Gamery was designed by gamers, for gamers — with care, attention to detail, and a focus on real-world use. It’s your backlog tracker, gaming journal, collection manager, wishlist, and companion — all in one clean, customizable, powerful app. Download Gamery today and take control of your gaming universe.   • Solid Game Tracker with Potential

    07/24/2023

    joshvickerson

    If you’re anything like me, you have more games than you have time to play, and this is an awesome way to keep track. The premium features are a must-have, in particular the automatic importing. Not having to manually keep track of which achievements I’ve completed is a huge selling point.It’s not perfect, though. The UI can feel clunky at times (story progress input, for example). Each game page also makes a few assumptions that don’t hold up for every game. Again, story progress comes to mind, as it’s not relevant to some categories of games, such as simulation or sandbox games. With the premium subscription the page can be customized for each game, so it’s a mostly correctable issue, though it can make this less useful for those kinds of games. I’d love the ability to enter custom tasks or something to keep track of projects in sandbox games.Overall, the app is solid, and I look forward to seeing it continue to be developed.
