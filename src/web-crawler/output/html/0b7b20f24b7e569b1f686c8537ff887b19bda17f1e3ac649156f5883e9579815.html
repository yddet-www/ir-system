Reideen The Brave

Originally broadcast in 1975, Reideen introduced a fresh perspective to the robot anime genre. At its core is the mysterious giant robot Reideen and its deep connection with the protagonist, Akira Hibiki, who becomes one with it. The series captivated audiences with its profound and enigmatic human drama. Notably, it also pioneered a full-scale toy collaboration during productiona groundbreaking move at the time that went on to become a standard practice in anime merchandising.

Combattler V

Directed by Tadao Nagahama, this landmark robot anime marked the beginning of what would later be known as the Romantic Robot Anime Trilogy. A true classic, it skillfully blends intense mecha action with deep psychological storytelling, exploring the emotions and motivations of both heroes and villains. Notably, COMBATTLER Va robot formed by combining five individual mechabecame a massive hit as a toy and left a lasting impact on the design of future combining robot series.

Aura Battler Dunbine

Set in Byston Well, a mystical world between the earth and the sea, this 1980s animated TV series blends medieval European aesthetics with the thrilling spectacle of living, creature-like giant robots. The result was a powerful and unforgettable experience that left a lasting mark on the robot anime genre. Directed by Yoshiyuki Tomino, the series not only captivated viewers with its unique setting and dramatic storytelling but also inspired a wide range of novels and anime set in the same universe. The saga of Byston Well became one of Tominos defining masterpieces.

New Story Of AURA BATTLER Dunbine

This original video animation takes place several hundred years after the 1983 broadcast of Yoshiyuki Tominos acclaimed Aura Battler Dunbine. Spanning three episodes, it preserves the essence of the original TV series while embracing a more fantastical tone. Notably, the Aura Battlerdesigned by Yutaka Izubuchiis portrayed not merely as a robot, but as a towering knight or mystical beast. This imaginative approach had a significant influence on the design of later mecha in the genre.

Heavy Metal L-Gaim

Set in the Pentagona World, a universe of five planets, this series follows a group of young rebels rising up against oppressive rulers. With character and mecha designs by Mamoru Nagano, the show stood out for its groundbreaking visual style. The humanoid weapon known as Heavy Metal remains iconic, especially for its innovative features like the internal MORVABUL F-LAME and the massive Buster Launcher, a firearm longer than the unit itself. These bold design choices left a lasting impact across anime, games, and toy design.

Mobile Suit Z Gundam

A direct sequel to Mobile Suit Gundam, this series is set seven years after the One Year War and significantly expands the Universal Century timeline. It became a cornerstone of the Gundam franchise, deepening its lore and influence. From 2005 to 2006, director Yoshiyuki Tomino reimagined the story as a theatrical trilogy, which was met with widespread acclaim and commercial success.

Mobile Suit Gundam Char's Counterattack

This theatrical film captures the climactic final confrontation between fated rivals Amuro Ray and Char Aznable. While their intense rivalry forms the emotional core of the story, the film also weaves in a rich tapestry of human drama and intricately detailed mechanical design. The result is a work of unparalleled depth and craftsmanshipwidely regarded as one of the most polished and influential entries in the Gundam saga.

Mobile Fighter G Gundam

The first entry in the Alternative Series, this Gundam installment breaks away from the Universal Century timeline and introduces a bold new concept: the Gundam Fight, a global tournament where Gundams represent different nations in battle. This unexpected twist captivated audiences and brought in a fresh wave of fans. The series also gained popularity for its vibrant cast of charactersespecially the intense showdown between the protagonist, Domon, and his master, the legendary Undefeated of the East, which remains iconic in Gundam history.

Mobile Suit Gundam Wing: Endless Waltz

This original video animation series serves as a sequel to the 1995 TV broadcast, offering a deeper resolution to the emotional arcs that couldnt be fully explored in the original. With redesigned mobile suits and a more focused narrative, the series was well-received by fans and critics alike. Its success also extended to merchandise, with the accompanying plastic model kits becoming a major hit. In 1998, a special edition featuring new scenes was released in theaters, further cementing its popularity.

Mobile Suit Gundam SEED Destiny

A sequel to Mobile Suit Gundam SEED, this series takes place two years after the events of the original and follows a new protagonist, Shin Asuka. As he navigates a world still shaped by conflict, familiar characters from the previous series return, bringing their own ideals and convictions into the mix. The intersecting paths of old and new heroes create a compelling narrative that has been widely praised for its emotional depth and dynamic storytelling.

Mobile Suit Gundam the Witch from Mercury Season 1

The story begins when Suletta Mercury, a transfer student from the planet Mercury, arrives at Asticassia School of Technologyan academy operated by the Benerit Group, the largest force in the mobile suit industry. One of the series standout features is the intense duel system, where mobile suits face off in high-stakes battles within the school setting. Spanning 24 episodes across the PROLOGUE, Season 1, and Season 2, the series captivated audiences with its striking character portrayals and dramatic musical score. It was especially well received by newcomers to the Gundam universe, drawing in a fresh generation of fans.

Mazinkaiser VS the Great General of Darkness

This one-episode sequel to the original video animation series Mazinger Kaiser continues the saga of Mazinger with a brand-new storyline. It portrays the intense battle between the invading Mycenae Empire and the Mazinger Army. As humanity teeters on the brink of defeat, the dramatic turnaround led by Koji Kabuto and the formidable Mazinger Kaiserthrough their overwhelming presence and fierce combatstands out as a major highlight of this title.

Getter Robo Arc

As one of the iconic sagas created by Go Nagai and Ken Ishikawa, the final chapter of the original Getter Robo mangaintroducing a new generation of Getter Robohas at last been adapted into a film.

Despite the untimely passing of co-creator Ken Ishikawa, this once-unfinished masterpiece has been brought to life on screen, reigniting the passion of longtime Getter fans.

Developed by a team of veteran creators with deep ties to Dynamic Planning, the film not only preserves the spirit of the original but also boldly forges a new chapter in the Getter Robo legacy.

Majestic Prince

Produced by Doga Kobo Orange, this project aims to usher in a new era of robot anime through a bold mix of media and innovative storytelling techniques. The high-quality CG battle sequences and serious narrative stand in striking contrast to the vibrant, popular characters. Three years after the original broadcast concluded, a completely new theatrical version was brought to life, capturing both fresh energy and fan anticipation.

Macross Delta

Macross is a beloved series known for its signature blend of songs, variable fighters, and love triangles. Set eight years after the previous film, Macross Frontier, this installment introduces the Tactical Sound Unit Walkrethe first five-member group in the franchisewhose songs gained popularity far beyond the film itself. Featuring high-speed battles with transforming fighters, the movie honors the series traditions while embracing its evolution. With its dynamic visuals, emotional depth, and musical energy, this film is packed with highlights for both longtime fans and newcomers alike.


Where to Watch: Hulu

First Full Episode: YouTube

Macross Delta: Passionate Walkre

This theatrical adaptation of the TV series Macross Delta reimagines the story through the lens of the Tactical Sound Unit Walkre. Featuring newly animated scenes, the film offers a fresh perspective that sets it apart from the original series. Following the TV broadcast, Walkre gained even greater popularity, and the film further solidified their status with the debut of new songs that captivated fans.

Code Geass: Lelouch of the Rebellion III Glorification

The third and final installment in the three-part theatrical compilation of the TV anime series Code Geass: Lelouch of the Rebellion and R2, which originally aired from 2006 to 2008. While certain elements differ from the TV version, the film faithfully portrays the culmination of the Zero RequiemLelouchs ultimate sacrifice to bring lasting peace to the world.

Code Geass Lelouch of the Re;surrection

This brand-new theatrical film follows the three-part compilation series Code Geass: Lelouch of the Rebellion IIII and was released in theaters in 2019. Set in a world at peace after the events of Zero Requiem, the story takes place more than a decade after the TV series conclusion, depicting the unexpected return of Lelouch and the emergence of a new conflict. While offering a sense of closure to the saga of Geass and Lelouch, the films ending also hints at the beginning of a new chapter in the Code Geass universe.

Godzilla Singular Point

This brand-new TV anime series reimagines the legendary special effects franchise Godzilla in animated form. The story follows a clash between two brilliant minds and a robot named Jet Jaguar, equipped with a dialogue interface, as they battle monstrous threats emerging from a mysterious phenomenon known as Red Dust. With the world on the brink of destruction, the overwhelming presence of Godzilla, brought to life through powerful animation, anchors a gripping science fiction drama that explores forces beyond human understanding.


Where to Watch: Netflix

SSSS.DYNAZENON

Created by the team behind SSSS.GRIDMAN, this new TV anime series introduces the revived monster user Gauma, who awakens after 5,000 years to battle alongside his allies and the giant robot Dynazenon. While based on the tokusatsu classic Denko Choujin Gridman, the series is not a direct sequel to SSSS.GRIDMAN, though it shares elements of the same universe. Packed with homages to past works, the series has sparked lively discussion among fans and stands as a bold new chapter in the Gridman universe.


Where to Watch: CrunchyRoll
